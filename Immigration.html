Immigration FAQs

Answers to common questions about our immigration services and processes.

How long does the immigration process typically take?

Processing times vary significantly depending on the country, visa type, and individual circumstances. Simple tourist visas may take 2-4 weeks, while residency applications can take several months to years. Complex cases like citizenship by investment typically take 6-12 months. During our consultation, we provide realistic timelines for your specific case based on current processing times and your unique situation.

What documents will I need to provide?

Document requirements vary by visa type and destination country but typically include:

  • Valid passport with sufficient validity
  • Passport-sized photographs meeting specific requirements
  • Proof of financial means (bank statements, employment letters)
  • Health certificates and medical examination results
  • Police clearance certificates from countries lived in
  • Purpose-specific documents (acceptance letters, job offers, etc.)

We provide a customized checklist after assessing your case to ensure you gather all necessary documentation correctly.

Can you help if my visa application was previously refused?

Yes, we specialize in overcoming visa refusals. Our process includes:

  1. Detailed analysis of refusal reasons and your previous application
  2. Identifying and addressing all deficiencies in documentation
  3. Strengthening your new application with additional evidence
  4. Preparing persuasive legal arguments when appropriate
  5. Representing you in appeals processes if necessary

Many clients come to us after unsuccessful attempts elsewhere, and we have a strong track record of turning refusals into approvals.

Do you work with investment immigration programs?

Absolutely. We're highly experienced with all major investment immigration programs worldwide, including:

  • Golden Visa programs (Portugal, Spain, Greece, etc.)
  • Citizenship by investment (Caribbean, Malta, Turkey, etc.)
  • Entrepreneur and business investor visas (USA E-2, UK Innovator, Canada Start-up Visa)
  • Real estate investment residency programs

We help select the right program for your budget and goals while ensuring full compliance with all requirements. Our financial and legal experts work together to structure your investment optimally.

How are your fees structured?

We offer transparent, competitive pricing with several options:

  • Fixed-fee packages: For straightforward cases where we can predict the work involved
  • Hourly rates: For complex or unpredictable cases requiring flexibility
  • Success-based fees: For certain investment immigration cases

Government fees are always separate and clearly outlined upfront. After our consultation, we provide a detailed fee estimate with no obligations. We never surprise clients with hidden fees.
