Program Overview

The St. Lucia Citizenship by Investment Program was established in 2015 and offers investors and their families the opportunity to obtain St. Lucian citizenship through a government-approved investment. The program is known for its efficiency, transparency, and the high quality of its due diligence process.

St. Lucia is an English-speaking Caribbean nation with a stable democracy and strong economic growth. Citizenship provides visa-free access to key business and travel destinations including the UK, Schengen Zone, and Hong Kong.

Investment Options

  • National Economic Fund: $100,000 single applicant (minimum)
  • Real Estate: $300,000 minimum investment in approved projects
  • Government Bonds: $250,000 minimum investment (5-year holding)
  • Enterprise Project: $3.5 million minimum investment (joint ventures $6 million)

Requirements

  • Clean criminal record
  • Proof of legitimate source of funds
  • Good health
  • Minimum age of 18 years

Program Overview

The St. Kitts & Nevis Citizenship by Investment Program is the world's longest-running economic citizenship program, established in 1984. It offers investors and their families the opportunity to obtain citizenship through either a contribution to the Sustainable Growth Fund or through real estate investment.

St. Kitts & Nevis is a stable democracy with a strong tourism-based economy. The citizenship provides excellent global mobility including visa-free access to the UK, EU Schengen zone, Russia, and many other key business and travel destinations.

Investment Options

  • Sustainable Growth Fund: $250,000 single applicant (minimum)
  • Real Estate: $400,000 minimum investment in approved projects
  • Public Benefit Option: $175,000 minimum contribution

Requirements

  • Clean criminal record
  • Proof of legitimate source of funds
  • Medical certificate

Citizenship by Investment Overview

Understanding the Process

×

What is Citizenship by Investment?

Citizenship by Investment (CBI) programs allow qualified individuals to obtain citizenship in a country by making a significant economic contribution. These government-approved programs provide a legal pathway to second citizenship and often include benefits like visa-free travel, tax optimization, and family security.

How the Process Works

The typical citizenship by investment process involves:

  1. Program Selection: Choosing the right program based on your budget, timeline, and objectives
  2. Application Preparation: Gathering required documents and completing forms
  3. Due Diligence: Background checks conducted by the government
  4. Investment: Making the approved financial contribution
  5. Approval: Receiving citizenship certificate and passport

Key Considerations

  • Legitimacy: Only consider government-approved programs
  • Due Diligence: All reputable programs conduct background checks
  • Professional Guidance: Work with licensed agents for proper advice
  • Tax Implications: Consult tax professionals about your specific situation
